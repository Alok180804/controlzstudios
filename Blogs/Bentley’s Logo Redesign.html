Bentley’s Logo Redesign: The Flying B Takes a Bold New Flight

Bentley, a name that echoes legacy and luxury, recently unveiled a subtle yet powerful logo redesign. The Flying B now soars with more clarity, cleaner lines, and a digital-first philosophy—without losing an ounce of heritage. Here’s what changed, and why it matters.

The Evolution of the Winged B

The original Bentley logo, crafted by artist F. Gordon Crosby, showcased a bold ‘B’ inside sweeping wings—a nod to Bentley's early aviation ties. Initially asymmetrical and ornate, the logo evolved under Rolls-Royce ownership into a more balanced form with 10 feathers, and again in the 1990s to a version closer to its roots.

The Flying B: A Sculptural Icon

The Flying B hood ornament began as an upright brass badge with rigid wings. Over time, it adapted to Bentley’s sleeker cars—first as a single wing, then flowing twin wings. In 2002, a new Winged B was introduced. Safety regulations later required retractable versions, which reappeared in 2006.

In 2019, for Bentley’s Centenary Year, designer Hoe Young Hwang reimagined the Flying B—sleek, minimalist, and inspired by the owl. Hand-polished and built for modern luxury, it’s a sculptural statement of movement and elegance.

What’s New in Bentley’s Logo Design?

While it might appear unchanged at a glance, the logo redesign is a masterclass in subtlety. Key updates include:

  • Feather reduction: Streamlined wings for a cleaner silhouette
  • Sharper typography: The iconic ‘B’ has refined curves and stroke balance
  • Thicker lines: Improved visibility at smaller sizes
  • Digital-readiness: Optimized for mobile, apps, and screens

Why Bentley Updated Its Logo

The update isn’t about changing identity—it’s about clarity. In a world where logos appear on smartwatch screens and smartphone dashboards, fine details must perform flawlessly at every size. The redesign ensures Bentley remains instantly recognizable, everywhere.

What the Flying B Still Stands For

The Flying B remains a symbol of speed, class, and motion. It captures Bentley’s aviation roots, its performance DNA, and now, its commitment to evolving luxury. Even in its new form, the Flying B keeps its soul intact.

Design Lessons from Bentley

1. Don’t Overdesign

If your logo has equity, refine it—don’t reinvent it. Bentley made minimal changes with maximum effect.

2. Think Digital First

A logo today must work on everything from hood ornaments to favicons. Design with responsiveness in mind.

3. Respect the Past, Embrace the Future

Bentley’s logo still carries its heritage, but with a cleaner, sharper edge suited for 2025 and beyond.

Final Thoughts

Bentley’s logo redesign is a quiet power move—a fine-tuned refresh that future-proofs a century-old icon. It proves that great design doesn’t shout; it whispers with confidence. As a design studio, we see this as a model for thoughtful, digital-ready brand evolution.
